Affect human health

October 13, 2019

Increasing temperatures along with floods and droughts have created favorable conditions for infectious animals such as mosquitoes, ticks, mice to multiply and spread diseases, which are harmful to the health of many parts of the population all over the world.

Climate change has a direct impact on human health

Through the exchange of material and energy between the human body and the surrounding environment, resulting in changes in physiology, habits, and ability to like. Prolonged heat waves, increased air temperature, lead to an increase in some risks for old age, people with cardiovascular disease, neurological diseases, allergies. In addition, over time, heat waves in many countries around the world are increasing, which has claimed the lives of many people.

The indirect impact of climate change on human health through pathogens:

Increases the likelihood of outbreaks and spread of diseases such as influenza A / H1N1, influenza A / H5N1, diarrhea and cholera, etc...

Futhermore, climate change increases the likelihood of some tropical diseases such as malaria, dengue fever, Japanese Encephalitis.

symptoms of dengue contagious disease

Accelerates the growth and development of many types of bacteria and insects(flies, mosquitoes, mice, fleas, ticks).

The WHO has reported that dangerous epidemics are spreading in more places in the world than ever before. Areas that used to have a cold climate now also have tropical diseases. Every year, about 150,000 people die from climate-related illnesses, from heart disease due to extreme temperatures to respiratory problems and diarrhea. Also related to this problem, the research results of the Central Institute of Parasitology and Insect showed that the disease leading to death due to the impact of climate change is caused by excessive temperatures. Increasing people with heart disease, the risk of death in the elderly and young children is higher due to reduced resistance to the body's disease.

Climate change also makes natural disasters, especially storms, floods, droughts, earthquakes, tsunamis increase in frequency, intensity and scale. Typically, in recent years, due to climate change, there have been more and more destructive storms, destroying houses, buildings, crops and killing thousands of people.

In addition, increasing greenhouse gas emissions are a major cause of global warming. Destruction of the ozone layer leads to an increase in the level of ultraviolet radiation on the ground. This has caused skin cancer and eye diseases.
